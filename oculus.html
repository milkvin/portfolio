CHAPTER 1
The Oculus

Boarding the L train to work Monday morning, as she did every day, Zoey took a sip of her double-shot latte and thought about the photograph.

She thought about it for the full forty minutes it took to travel west and then south, from Brooklyn to her last stop in Lower Manhattan, and she thought about it as she stood to exit the train along with a thousand other passengers.

What was it about that photograph?

The subway car doors opened and Zoey became a drop in the ocean commuters as it poured through Fulton Center, the hub where nearly every subway line in Lower Manhattan converged. The wave carried her along through the gray-tiled passageway and out into the huge open space below the WorldTrade Center, where Zoey stopped, rooted in place, as people flowed around her. She glanced up at the cavernous ceiling. It looked like the ribs of an enormous birdcast in white steel, a phoenix risen from the ashes of 9/11.

She began moving again, feeling the hugeness of the place as she walked. Six hundred feet of pure white Italian marble. It was like being in a gigantic cathedral.

The Oculus. Gateway to one of the most famous memorials and tourist destinations in the world. Zoey passed through it every day—twice, in fact: once on the way to work and then again on the way home—yet she’d never really stopped to take it in.

She entered the white marble–lined West Concourse passageway, with its enormous LED wall display to her left, nearly a football field in length. Normally she ignored the constant rotation of advertisements and public service announcements, intent only on getting to the escalator. Today the image splashed across the big screen made Zoey stop in her tracks once more.

The picture showed a fishing boat, complete with crew and nets—very much like the boat in that photograph, the one she couldn’t get out of her mind. Only, rather than rocking in the water at dockside, this boat sat stranded in the middle of a desert.

Strange, thought Zoey. Strange, and strangely unsettling.

As she watched, giant letters scrolled across the image, spelling out a message:

“If you don’t know where you’re going, you might not like where you end up.”

Moments later the image dissolved, replaced by more ads.

Zoey walked on.

Reaching the end of the passageway, she stepped onto the escalator, which carried her two stories up and into the sunlit glass atrium. She walked outside and turned back toward West Street, the sun in her eyes, to face the building where she worked. One World Trade Center, the tallest building in the WesternHemisphere. This was her daily routine. She loved standing in this spot, tipping her head way back and looking straight up, trying to see the top of the enormous tower as it stretched toward the sky.

Today, though, her mind was elsewhere.

“If you don’t know where you’re going, you might not like where you end up.”

It was an ad for something—insurance company, car company, travel app, she couldn’t quite remember what. Hadn’t Jessica had something to do with that slogan? It seemed to her that this was one of Jess’s accounts, whatever it was they were advertising. Yet this morning somehow it felt like a personal message directed right at Zoey. And it gnawed at her.

Just like that photograph. The one she couldn’t get out of her mind.

She suddenly remembered the latte in her left hand and took a sip. It had gone cold.

Normally she would now cross the street, enter the building, and take the elevator up to her office on the thirty-third floor. Today she diverted from her usual path. After crossing over West Street, she took a sharp right, heading away from One World Trade, and walked toward the reflecting pools, the two enormous square fountains built on the precise footprints of the original TwinTowers, bordered by short black marble walls with an endless stretch of names carved into their top surfaces.

The 9/11 Memorial.

She stopped at the north pool and looked down at the surging water below.Felt the surface of the marble and read the first dozen names. There were so many of them. Thousands of people had died here, in those dark days of September2001. Zoey had been in grade school then. She glanced over at the great ribbedwings of the Oculus jutting up among the skyscrapers a block away.

Why did everything look so different to her today?

“If you don’t know where you’re going, you might not like where you end up.”

Where exactly was it that Zoey was going? Where exactly did she expect to endup?

Had she ever really thought about that before?

A man stopped for a split second to glare at the watch on his wrist, then hurried on. Zoey stirred. She was going to be late for work.

She started to turn away to head back toward One World Trade Center—but something held her in place. Instead, she stepped over to one of the nearby concrete benches and sat down, cold latte in hand, as the stream of tourists,commuters, and locals flowed past. She spoke softly, to no one but herself:

“What am I doing with my life?”






BAGIAN 1
Oculus

Seperti pada hari biasanya, perempuan itu mengendarai Kereta L ke tempat kerjanya, Zoey meneguk latte double-shot miliknya dan memikirkan tentang foto.

Ia memikirkan hal tersebut sepanjang empat puluh menit perjalanan, dari barat, lalu selatan, dari Brooklyn ke tempat tujuan akhirnya di Lower Manhattan, dan Ia juga memikirkannya ketika hendak keluar dari kereta bersama dengan penumpang lainnya.

Ada apa dengan foto itu?

Pintu kereta terbuka dan Zoey menyelami lautan manusia yang melewati Fulton Center, pusat berkumpulnya jalur kereta di Lower Manhattan. Lautan tadi membawanya mengalir melewati jalan berlantai keramik abu-abu, lalu ke sebuah ruang terbuka di bawah World Trade Center, yang mana Zoey berhenti, terdiam di tempat, ketika para manusia tetap berlalu tak henti. Ia menatap ke langit-langit yang terbuka. Langit-langit itu terlihat seperti sebuah burung besi yang besar, burung phoenix yang muncul dari kejadian kelam 9/11.

Ia mulai melangkah kembali, merasakan betapa luasnya tempat tersebut. Marbel Italia asli membentang seluas enam ratus kaki. Rasanya seperti berada di sebuah katedral yang besar.

Oculus. Pintu gerbang dari salah satu monumen dan destinasi terpopuler di dunia. Zoey melewati gerbang itu setiap hari—dua kali, faktanya: sekali untuk pergi kerja dan sekali lagi saat akan pulang—tapi Ia tidak pernah berhenti untuk mengunjunginya.

Ia memasuki jalan yang beralaskan marbel putih yang ditata rapi, dengan layar LED di sebelah kirinya, kira-kira panjang jalan itu seperti lapangan bola. Biasanya Ia tidak akan peduli dengan iklan maupun pengumuman, hanya memikirkan tujuan untuk menaiki eskalator saja. Hari ini, sebuah gambar muncul di salah satu iklan dan membuat Zoey terhenti kembali.

Dalam gambar itu terdapat sebuah perahu nelayan, lengkap dengan kru dan jaring—sepertinya perahu dalam foto itulah yang tidak bisa Ia lupakan. Bedanya, kapal itu tidak sedang berada di dermaga, tapi terdampar di tengah gurun pasir.

Aneh, pikir Zoey. Aneh, dan aneh yang mengganggu.

Ketika Ia sedang melihat gambar tersebut, muncul tulisan berukuran besar, yang mengatakan:

“Jika kamu tidak mengetahui tujuanmu, kelak kamu mungkin tidak akan menyukai tempatmu berada.”

Beberapa saat kemudian, gambar itu menghilang dan berganti dengan iklan lain.

Zoey kembali berjalan.

Setelah sampai di ujung jalan, Ia melangkahkan kaki ke eskalator yang membawanya melewati dua lantai dan ke dalam sebuah atrium dengan kaca. Ia melangkah keluar dan berjalan menuju West Street, sinar matahari menyilaukan matanya ketika Ia sedang memandang gedung kantornya. One World Trade Center, gedung tertinggi di Western Hemisphere. Ini adalah rutinitasnya. Ia suka berdiri di titik ini, menengok ke atas, mencoba melihat titik tertinggi dari menara yang menjulang ke langit ini.

Akan tetapi, hari ini pikirannya berada di tempat lain.

“Jika kamu tidak mengetahui tujuanmu, kelak kamu mungkin tidak akan menyukai tempatmu berada.”

Itu adalah sebuah iklan—perusahaan asuransi, perusahaan mobil, aplikasi perjalanan, Ia tidak bisa mengingatnya. Bukankah Jessica berhubungan dengan slogan itu? Ia merasa kata-kata itu ada di akunnya Jess, sesuatu yang sedang mereka iklankan. Namun, pagi ini, rasanya seperti sebuah pesan yang langsung tertuju untuk Zoey. Dan itu mengganggunya.

Hanya seperti foto itu. Foto yang tidak bisa Ia keluarkan dari kepalanya.

Ia langsung teringat latte di tangan kirinya dan meneguknya sekali. Sudah dingin.

Biasanya Ia akan menyebrang jalan, memasuki gedung, dan menaiki elevator menuju ruangannya di lantai tiga puluh tiga. Sekarang dia melalui jalan yang berbeda. Setelah menyeberangi West Street, Ia berbelok ke kanan, menjauh dari One World Trade, dan berjalan menuju sebuah kolam, dua air mancur yang besar itu dibangun tepat di atas tanah bekas Twin Towers, dengan dibatasi tembok pendek berlapiskan keramik hitam dan terukir nama-nama di atasnya.

Monumen 9/11.

Ia berhenti di bagian utara dan melihat air yang sedang bergelombang. Merasakan permukaan keramik dan membaca puluhan nama di atasnya. Ada banyak sekali. Ribuan orang telah meninggal di sini, dalam hari suram pada September 2001. Waktu itu Zoey masih berada di bangku sekolah. Ia menatap ke sayap bergaris dari Oculus menonjol di antara para pencakar langit di blok itu.

Mengapa hari ini semua tampak berbeda baginya?

“Jika kamu tidak mengetahui tujuanmu, kelak kamu mungkin tidak akan menyukai tempatmu berada.”

Apa yang seharusnya menjadi tujuan Zoey? Di mana tempat yang Ia harapkan?

Apakah dia pernah memikirkannya?

Seorang laki-laki berhenti sejenak untuk melihat jam tangan di pergelangannya, lalu bergegas pergi. Zoey bangkit. Ia akan terlambat untuk kerja.

Ia mulai kembali berjalan menuju One World Trade Center—tapi ada yang menahannya. Ia memutuskan untuk berhenti di dekat sebuah bangku beton dan duduk. Dengan latte yang telah dingin di tangannya, ketika turis, orang-orang terus berlalu-lalang. Ia berbicara pelan, kepada dirinya sendiri:

“Apa yang kulakukan dengan hidupku?”
